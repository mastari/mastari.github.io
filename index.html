Entry #34: Plagiarism
The Concept of Plagiarism By Unknown Writer
What does it mean if your work is identical to someone else's? Did you create the writing and all the ideas were yours? Whichever party you may be apart of, you are participating in a case of plagiarism. Writing is the opportunity to take all the thoughts in your mind and transfer them into a physical embodiment. The Oxford Dictionary defines plagiarism as: “The practice of taking someone else's work or ideas and passing them off as one's own.” This definition encompasses not only the exact text that was written but also minor adjustments such as insertion and truncation of sentences or modifying parts of speech. The reason why those changes qualify as plagiarism is because the plagiarizer has not described their own ideas entirely in the work. Instead the plagiarizer took the ideas “...passing them off as one’s own...” to gain credit for the piece while avoiding to put in the work. The problem for the plagiarizer is that they don’t acquire any new writing skills and they never get to express their true ideas. On the other hand, the person who is responsible for the essay never gets credited for their work and they may be the one that gets mistaken for plagiarizing. In the end, plagiarism is a messy situation all around and it is better for everyone if you take the initiative to do the work honestly.
